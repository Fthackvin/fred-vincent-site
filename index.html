Send me a problem

How we start

There are three rough shapes the work can take, depending on what you need.

Two to six weeks

Quick Win Sprint

Two to six weeks reviewing what you've built and shipping concrete improvements faster than an agency would. You get three or more specific recommendations, how to measure them, and the bet that has the most upside. If helpful, I can also design and ship the interventions.

Most useful when

You have a specific product or surface you want to improve, with a metric or outcome in mind.

Three to four weeks

Exploratory

Three to four weeks going deeper than a sprint allows for. We look at what your product is really trying to do, where the gaps are between intention and execution, and what direction has the strongest claim. The output is a written articulation with sketches and references.

Most useful when

You sense your product could be more than it currently is but the answer isn't obvious yet.

Six to eight weeks

Step change

Six to eight weeks finding a direction that changes the rules of the space your product is in, not just what's made inside it. Builds on the work above. The output is a developed creative direction, ready for your team to build against.

Most useful when

The obvious moves have been considered and none of them feel right.

Six months minimum

Ongoing creative direction

A monthly arrangement after one of the engagements above, where I stay attached to your team to make sure the direction we found gets built without losing its substance.

Usually two days a month split between reviews of work in progress, a monthly call, and being available for the harder questions as they come up. I work this way with a small number of teams at a time so the attention is real.

Most useful when

You've commissioned an Exploratory or Step change engagement and want to keep me involved as you build, or you want senior creative judgment available without the cost of a full-time hire.

  1. 1. A 30-minute call. We talk about what you're working on, who's involved, and what you're trying to figure out. You ask whatever you want about how I work. By the end we both know whether to keep going.
  2. 2. A short proposal, within a few days. Scope, timeline, fee, deliverables. If anything doesn't feel right, we adjust before signing.
  3. 3. Work starts within one or two weeks. For shorter engagements that means I'm in your product and talking to your team. For longer engagements we agree a rhythm, usually one or two days a week embedded.
  4. 4. We talk if anything changes. Scope shifts, priorities move, a different shape of work would be more useful. I'd rather adjust openly than push through a bad fit.
Common questions Hide questions

What if I don't know what shape of engagement I want?

Common, and fine. The first call is partly to figure that out. I'll tell you honestly which format fits, or if none of them do.

How do you handle confidentiality?

NDA on request, signed before specifics. I don't write about client work without explicit permission.

Do you take equity instead of fees?

Mostly no. Cash keeps the relationship clean. Open to partial equity alongside reduced cash for early-stage work that's genuinely meaningful, not as default.

What if it isn't a good fit?

We don't proceed. Better to decline at scoping than underdeliver. If something feels wrong mid-engagement, we talk and either adjust or close out cleanly.
